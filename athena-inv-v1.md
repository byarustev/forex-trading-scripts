# Refined Trading Strategy with FVG Inversion Entry Criteria

This strategy combines top-down analysis, market structure, and Fair Value Gap (FVG) inversion to provide precise entry points based on market shifts. The **1H inverted FVG** confirms that price is respecting the Point of Interest (POI); the entry does not have to fall inside the supply or demand zone.

---

## 1. Mark Swing Lows and Swing Highs on the 4H Timeframe

**Swing Lows:** Identify significant swing lows where price has previously reversed upward (support).

**Swing Highs:** Identify significant swing highs where price has previously reversed downward (resistance).

**Action:** Mark all swing highs and swing lows on your 4H chart. These will help establish key market levels and trend structure.

---

## 2. Identify Break of Structure (BoS)

BoS occurs when price breaks through a swing high (in a bullish setup) or a swing low (in a bearish setup), signaling a potential trend continuation or reversal.

**Action:** After identifying swing highs and swing lows, assess where the BoS occurs. This helps you define whether the market is shifting from bullish to bearish or bearish to bullish.

---

## 3. Define Directional Bias Based on BoS

- **Bullish Bias:** If price breaks above a swing high, the market shows signs of an uptrend — look for demand zones near strong lows to buy.
- **Bearish Bias:** If price breaks below a swing low, the market shows signs of a downtrend — look for supply zones near strong highs to sell.

**Action:** Confirm the directional bias based on whether the BoS occurred to the upside (bullish) or downside (bearish).

---

## 4. Wait for Price to Revisit a POI (Main or Continuation)

### Main POI (fresh zone that caused the BoS)

The **Point of Interest (POI)** must be a **fresh zone** — i.e. a supply zone (bearish) or demand zone (bullish) **where price has not yet touched before**. This is the zone from which the move originated after the BoS. Wait for price to retest this zone before looking for an entry.

### Continuation POIs (OB + FVG left behind after the move)

After a BoS, price often leaves **new fresh zones** in its wake: an **order block** (OB — the candle before the impulse) followed by a **Fair Value Gap** (FVG). Price frequently retraces to fill these areas. These act as **additional POIs** for taking **continuation** trades:

- **Order block (OB):** The range (high–low) of the 1H candle immediately before the FVG formed. On a retrace into this zone, look for the same entry logic (FVG inversion).
- **Continuation FVG zone:** The gap left by the FVG. When price retraces back into this gap, it can react and offer an entry on the next FVG inversion.

**Action:** Do not enter immediately after the BoS. Wait for price to revisit either (1) the main supply/demand zone (POI), or (2) a continuation POI (OB zone or FVG zone). Entry is then confirmed by 1H FVG inversion as below.

---

## 5. Look for FVG and Inversion (1H iFVG Confirms POI Respect)

The **entry model does not require the entry to be inside the supply or demand zone**. A more refined approach is used: the **inverted FVG on the 1H timeframe** confirms that price is **respecting the POI** (rejecting from the zone). Entry is then taken at the **middle of that 1H inverted FVG**, which may sit inside or outside the zone boundaries.

### For Bearish Bias

1. **Identify the Bullish FVG** on the 1H chart (imbalance from a sharp move up).
2. **Wait for inversion:** Price closes **below** the bullish FVG. This confirms that price is respecting the supply zone (POI) and that sentiment has shifted.
3. **Enter at the middle of the inverted FVG** (optimal risk–reward). This level may or may not fall inside the 4H supply zone.
4. **Target sell-side liquidity** (next support or weak low).

### For Bullish Bias

1. **Identify the Bearish FVG** on the 1H chart.
2. **Wait for inversion:** Price closes **above** the bearish FVG. This confirms that price is respecting the demand zone (POI).
3. **Enter at the middle of the inverted FVG.** Target **buy-side liquidity** (next resistance or weak high).

---

## 6. Risk Management

**Stop Loss:**

- **Bullish Setup:** Place the stop loss below the strong swing low (or demand zone).
- **Bearish Setup:** Place the stop loss above the strong swing high (or supply zone).

**Take Profit:**

- **Bullish Setup:** Set take profit at the weak high (next resistance level) or based on a minimum 2:1 risk-to-reward ratio. Prefer liquidity-based targets (buy-side liquidity) when clear.
- **Bearish Setup:** Set take profit at the weak low (next support level) or based on a minimum 2:1 risk-to-reward ratio. Prefer liquidity-based targets (sell-side liquidity) when clear.

---

## 7. Execute the Trade

### For Bullish Trade (Buy)

1. Confirm the bullish BoS and wait for price to revisit the demand zone (POI).
2. On 1H, wait for inversion of the bearish FVG (price closes above it).
3. Enter when price trades at or retraces to the **middle of the inverted bearish FVG**.
4. Optional: Confirm a bullish reversal pattern (e.g. bullish engulfing, pin bar).
5. Place stop loss below the strong low (or demand zone).
6. Take profit at the weak high (next resistance) or buy-side liquidity.

### For Bearish Trade (Sell)

1. Confirm the bearish BoS and wait for price to revisit the supply zone (POI).
2. On 1H, wait for inversion of the bullish FVG (price closes below it).
3. Enter when price trades at or retraces to the **middle of the inverted bullish FVG**.
4. Optional: Confirm a bearish reversal pattern (e.g. shooting star, bearish engulfing).
5. Place stop loss above the strong high (or supply zone).
6. Take profit at the weak low (next support) or sell-side liquidity.

---

## Summary of the Refined Strategy

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1** | Mark swing lows and swing highs on the **4H** chart. |
| **2** | Identify **Break of Structure (BoS)** — price breaking a swing high (bullish) or swing low (bearish). |
| **3** | Define **directional bias:** bullish → look for demand zones; bearish → look for supply zones. |
| **4** | Wait for price to **revisit a POI**: main (fresh 4H zone that caused the BoS) **or** continuation (OB zone or FVG zone left behind after the move). Do not enter on the break alone. |
| **5** | **Confirmation:** The **1H inverted FVG** confirms that price is respecting the POI. Entry is at the **middle of the 1H inverted FVG** — this may be inside or outside the zone. |
| **6** | **Targets:** Sell-side liquidity (bearish) or buy-side liquidity (bullish). Minimum 2:1 R:R when liquidity target is unclear. |
| **7** | **Risk:** Stop loss below swing low / demand zone (bullish) or above swing high / supply zone (bearish). |

**Key point:** The POI (4H supply/demand zone) is where we expect a reaction. The **1H iFVG** is the refined entry trigger that confirms "price is respecting this POI." The actual entry (middle of the 1H iFVG) does not have to be inside the supply or demand zone.
