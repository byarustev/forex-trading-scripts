So the lower-timeframe workflow becomes:
Wait for the weekly candle to close.
Classify it as disrespect or respect, and bullish or bearish.
Use that classification to define directional expectation and immediate target.
Drop to Daily first and look for valid PD arrays or POIs that exist inside that weekly candle's range.
Only if Daily does not provide a valid POI, then drop to 4H.
Mark those POIs as the candidate zones for the new week.
When the new week opens and price trades into one of those marked zones, wait for entry confirmation and then execute toward the predefined weekly target.
That constraint is actually excellent for turning this into an indicator because it reduces noise and makes the rules more programmable. It gives us a clear hierarchy:
Weekly decides bias and targets.
Daily is the first refinement layer for POIs.
4H is fallback refinement only.
All POIs must lie within the mother weekly candle.
So yes, this is clear, and it improves the design a lot.
What still needs to be defined before coding is mostly rule precision, not the overall concept. The biggest items are:

Phase 2: POI detection inside that weekly candle, using Daily first and 4H as fallback.

Phase 3: entry confirmation logic after price trades into the POI.
Phase 4: alerts, visuals, and trade management.
